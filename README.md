## üß† Project Description  

The **2025 Elections Voting Behavior Analysis of San Jose del Monte (SJDM), Bulacan** is a **data visualization and analytics project** that investigates the role of **demographics and generational turnout** in shaping electoral outcomes.  

SJDM has a population of **687,000** and **268,007 registered voters**. Out of **62 barangays**, **50 were analyzed**:  

- **26 barangays** from the First District  
- **24 barangays** from the Second District  

The project compares **pre-election surveys** with **official election results**, analyzing how **age, gender, and generational groups** influenced mayoral, vice-mayoral, senatorial, and councilor races in the city.  

---

## üíº Roles & Responsibilities  

- **Cleaned and processed** election data from **50 barangays**, fixing **120+ inconsistencies** and standardizing entries using **Google Sheets** and **Excel formulas**.  
  - Computed **measures of central tendency** and **variability**:  
    - **Gen Z** ‚Üí Highest variability (**stdev = 12.4**)  
    - **Boomers** ‚Üí Most consistent turnout (**stdev = 4.8**, **median = 61%**)  

- **Built an interactive Power BI dashboard** featuring:  
  - **4 generational filters** (Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X, Boomers)  
  - Drill-through analysis of **mayoral, vice-mayoral, senatorial, and councilor races**  
  - Comparative visuals of **dynastic vs. non-dynastic candidate support**  

- **Key findings**:  
  - **Millennials** averaged **58% support** for **non-dynastic candidates**  
  - **Boomers and Gen X** showed **65% support** for **incumbents**  
  - **Gen Z** exhibited **fragmented turnout** with shifting loyalties

## üìä Voting Behavior Analysis  

### 1. **Generational Voting Variability**  

| Generation  | Std. Dev. in Turnout | Median Support | Key Trend |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------|-----------|
| Gen Z       | **12.4**             | 48%            | High variability, shifting loyalties |
| Millennials | 9.1                  | 58%            | Higher non-dynastic candidate support |
| Gen X       | 6.2                  | 65%            | Stable support for incumbents |
| Boomers     | **4.8**              | 61%            | Most consistent turnout |

---

### 2. **Dynastic vs. Non-Dynastic Support**  

- **Millennials**: 58% ‚Üí Favored **non-dynastic reform candidates**  
- **Boomers & Gen X**: 65% ‚Üí Leaned toward **incumbents and dynastic figures**  
- **Gen Z**: Split between **new entrants** and **traditional parties**  

---

### 3. **District-Level Observations**  

- **First District (26 barangays):** Higher turnout consistency, strong dynastic support  
- **Second District (24 barangays):** More fragmented votes, driven by **youth voter diversity**  

---

## üéØ Key Takeaways  

- **Generational divides** had more impact than gender differences  
- **Youth voters (Gen Z + Millennials = 63% of electorate)** are decisive but inconsistent  
- **Data visualization** revealed clear **district-level differences**, aiding **future campaign strategies**
